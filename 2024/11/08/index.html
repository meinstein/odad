you loathe going outside,

but when you do,

you pause every two strides,

lost in the scents that surround you.


it takes a pull,

a force twenty times your weight,

to guide you back to me,

with an empty tank and a stubborn gait.


you ask for nothing, like charity's art,

wrapped in warmth, soft and fair,

a weighted blanket on my heart,

the best of burdens, beyond compare.
