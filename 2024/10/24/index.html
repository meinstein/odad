In the reptile house, the crocodile commands attention as parents and children crowd around the glass. Just behind them, by the iguana enclosure, an elderly lady leans on her walker, taking in the quiet corner.

She lifts her hand, holding it beside the iguana's twisted claws. “Oh,” she says with a chuckle, “you've got rheumatoid arthritis too, I see.” A child darts over, startling the iguana, which scurries away with surprising nimbleness, claws clacking on the rocks.

The lady laughs, steadies her grip, then turns toward the crocodile, ready to face down another relic of time.
